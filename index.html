Discover 3 Must-Visit Destinations in Busan

Busan's attractions range from centuries-old Buddhist temples dotting the city's mountains and coastline to pristine beaches with crystal-clear waters. This coastal city offers year-round activities for everyone – families can spend time at a seaside aquarium, shoppers can explore vibrant neighborhoods, and nature lovers can enjoy long hikes to panoramic viewpoints. The Buddhist sanctuaries scattered along Busan's coast and mountains feature stunning architecture that will captivate photographers.

1. Haedong Yonggungsa Temple

Haedong Yonggungsa Temple is a Buddhist temple located in the northeasternmost part of Busan. Built in 1376, it is one of the few temples in Korea constructed by the seaside – you can enjoy views of the East Sea on one side and beautiful mountains on the other.


Great for:

  • History

2. Beomeo-sa Temple

Beomeo-sa Temple is one of the largest sanctuaries in South Korea. It is located high on the eastern edge of Geumjeongsa Mountain, far from the hustle and bustle of the city. The temple's Daeungjeon Hall is a well-preserved example of Joseon Dynasty architecture.


Great for:

  • History

3. Yongdusan Park

Yongdusan Park, located in the heart of Busan, is home to some of the city's most important landmarks. You can enjoy spectacular views from the top of the 120-meter-high Busan Tower. The park features two museums – explore traditional musical instruments at the Museum of World Folk Instruments and over 80 Korean sailboats at the Exhibition Hall of World Model Boats.


Great for:

  • Couples
  • Families
  • Budget

The best things to do in Busan highlight the city's reputation as a major maritime hub in Asia. Often regarded as the essence of South Korea, you'll experience a unique atmosphere in terms of ethnic and cultural diversity, as the city welcomes a cosmopolitan crowd year-round.
